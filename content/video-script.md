# LinkedIn Video — Health Engine Walkthrough
~60 seconds. Run ./record.sh in terminal, narrate over it.

---

## Opening — clean screen (12s)

> "I'm about two months into a weight loss journey — down from 205, sitting around 194 this morning. Got a full panel done — 18 markers — lipids, metabolic, thyroid, inflammatory. Got the results back and honestly have no idea how it all fits together with my cut. So I built an engine to figure it out. Here's what a morning check-in looks like."

(check-in output appears)

---

## Engine responds — react to greeting (5s)

ENGINE SAYS:
"194.6 this morning — 7 lbs from target. Rate's stalled — let's look
at why. Sleep's at 6.5 hours — that's going to show up. HRV holding
strong though."

> "I didn't tell it any of that. It pulled my Garmin, my labs, my weight trend — and it already knows the rate stalled and sleep is probably why."

---

## Walk the data (10s)

> "Resting heart rate 50, HRV 64, ten thousand steps. Eighteen markers scored against actual CDC population data — not lab ranges, real percentiles. Lipids, ApoB, metabolic — all optimal. But I'm only seventy-four percent covered. Most people don't even know that's a number."

---

## Insights — linger here (10s)

> "This is where it clicks. Sleep under seven hours is dragging recovery. But HRV is still trending up because my zone two is strong enough to compensate. When you're in a deficit, everything talks to everything. This shows you how."

---

## Gaps (5s)

> "And the stuff I haven't measured. Family history — free. Highest leverage things I just hadn't done."

(dashboard opens in browser)

---

## Dashboard + Close (10s)

> "Same data, visual layer. All runs locally — nothing leaves your machine. This is what I wish I had when I got that first panel back. It's open source. Link in the comments."

---

## Notes
- Say it in your voice. These are guardrails, not a teleprompter.
